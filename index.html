This is NeuroSense

Over 20 million Americans live with sensory nerve damage to the extremeties. Clinicians need an affordable, objective way to detect and monitor sensory loss in patients over time. That's why we're building NeuroSense, a vibration-based device to quantitatively measure peripheral neuropathy.

Peripheral Neuropathy

  • An expensive problem

    Undetected neuropathy incurs $3k - $20k in healthcare costs.

  • Early detection

    80% of neuropathy-related amputations of extremeties are preventable with early detection.

  • Increased fall risk

    Neuropathy increases fall risk and injuries to extremeties by ~2.7x.

The Device

NeuroSense uses electromechanical vibration generation and digital signal control to objectively quantify sensory perception thresholds. During testing, the user places their finger on the device and presses a response button when vibration is perceived, allowing the system to interpret the vibration perception thresholds
These measurements are logged through integrated software that aggregates test data over time, providing clinicians and researchers with quantitative, repeatable metrics to assess disease progressiona and treatment response.
